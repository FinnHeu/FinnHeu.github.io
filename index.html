About Me

I am a climate physicist and oceanographer specializing in high-latitude climate dynamics and atmosphere-ocean-ice interactions. With a PhD from the University of Bremen, my work at the Alfred-Wegener-Institute focusses on understanding Arctic amplification, interannual variability in the ocean circulation, and the coupled climate system of the Barents Sea and Fram Strait. I have extensive experience in applying state-of-the-art ocean and climate models, in particular the Finite Volume Ocean and Sea Ice Model FESOM2.1, with strong programming skills in Python, MATLAB, and Fortran90. My research is documented in high-impact journals, and I thrive in interdisciplinary collaborations to address pressing climate challenges. Passionate about advancing Earth system modeling, I also enjoy sharing scientific insights through teaching, outreach, and engaging with diverse audiences.
